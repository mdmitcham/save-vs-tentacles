I turned *Mythic Bastionland* into a mythic Ancient Greek Odyssey.

Although I guess it's not really an Odyssey, since the party's still just exploring the one Realm. But! There are already ways to enter and exit other Realms -- Olympia, Kthonia, etc. So an odyssey it will be perhaps if the players take that route.

I'm calling the series *Sailing Under Wine-Dark Skies*. It'll consist of play reports and mechanics breakdowns and whatever else. If you go back to my other posts where I was trying to think through everything you can kind of see how I got from there to here. But I abandoned that. We've already had 5 sessions? Out of an expected 18-21.  And now I'm going to talk about the thing in its final form.

So here's the deal:

If you pick up Mythic Bastionland (recommended), you'll see that McDowell has included 72 unique knights, each delightfully mysterious and beautifully illustrated. Each one knighted by a unique Seer, each one with unique abilities and items, each one product of care and creative excellence.

Not me though.

I FOR SURE wasn't going to sit down and write 72 Greek heroes. Partly because I'm not familiar enough with the system to rebuild that much of it from scratch and have it hold together. And mostly because I'm not getting paid for the shit so it's going to be Reflavortown from here on out.

So what I did instead is I ripped all the beautiful and unique abilities and mechanics from each knight, apologies to Mr. McDowell, and threw each into an archetypal "bucket" that I'm calling a Path. NO THEY AREN'T THE SAME AS PBTA PLAYBOOKS SHUT UP.

So we've got:

The Path of the Wild
The Path of the Blade
The Path of Command
The Path of Prophecy

etc.

I think it was 10 total.

Each path rolls its own abilities, its own starting gear, and rolls to see whether you get a shieldbearer, a steed, or both. You select one path, then you roll to see what abilities, items, gear, followers, etc you get. In theory you could have PCs of the same Path, each with different and varied stuff.

"But Mason what about that rare and most flavorful of additions, the Passion of each knight?" I hear you cry.

Reflavored.

They're Epithets now.

Here are the Reasonable and Unquestionable Epithets of the Path of Divinity:

**The Entitled** — Restore SPI when you get something that you were denied.
**The Disruptive** — Restore SPI when you disrupt a place of peace.
**The Merciful** — Restore SPI when you accept a surrender or plea for forgiveness.
**The Fated** — Restore SPI when you give in to your fate.
**The Dithering** — Restore SPI when you are driven to theatrics trying to decide if you are a god or a mortal.
**The Mistaken** — Restore SPI when someone believes you to be a full god and you do not correct them.
**The Tender** — Restore SPI when you protect someone weaker than yourself at cost to yourself.
**The Ashamed** — Restore SPI when you are reminded of your mortal blood in front of others.

Here are the Unknowable and Perfectly Horrible Epithets of the Wild Path:

**The Feral** — Restore SPI when you reject an opportunity to sleep indoors, sleeping in nature instead.
**The Unlit** — Restore SPI when you refuse to go near fire.
**The Noisy** — Restore SPI when you howl at night for reasons discernible only to you.
**The Lean** — Restore SPI when you refuse food prepared by human hands.
**The Masked** — Restore SPI when you hide your face among groups of people.
**The Abstinent** — Restore SPI when you are offered meat and refuse, causing a scene.

We have a Meg the Noisy in our campaign.

Anyway, I made each player commit to one path at character creation and I'm thinking about forcing a player to keep the same path if they die. Hero with a thousand faces and all that. The Path of the Blade isn't really "your character," it's your lineage. Achilles dies, someone else picks up the Path of the Blade, and they're not Achilles's replacement (unless they are named Patroclus), they're just the next mighty warrior until they get murdered on by Hector.

I like the idea, but  I'm just not sure about whether we have enough randomness to justify it. We'll see how it holds up in play.

The other thing I did at character creation: a heroic deaths table. If any of your stats come in below 7 at chargen, you roll on the table and take a +1 to that stat. It means that your character already died once, before session one, and was reborn.

One player rolled on it four times -- off the top of my head, they drowned in a vat of wine once. Once they entered the bedchamber of Aphrodeia and were never seen again. I forget the rest but they are awesome. I will have more on this mechanic at a later date.

Overall I would say the Paths -- NOT PLAYBOOKS -- were a success. Each character felt different and the epithets did a lot of work.

Here were our starting Paths:

The Wild Path -- Meg the Noisy, with the ability to go unseen in the wilderness.
The Path of Prophecy -- Castor the Dismissed, with visions of the future and a preternatural sense of empathy.
The Path of Mageia -- Tautania the Unbothered, who commands the fell sorceries of Charm and Polymorph.
The Path of Divinity -- Persiclepius the Mistaken, he of supposed divine birth and a bronze arm and an eternal ball of yarn which attracts kittens.
The Path of Cunning -- Spiros the Fabricating, who can disguise himself and infiltrate places and institutions, or (which is more likely) run away.

The Path of Prophecy, Path of Mageia, and Path of Command are my own invention and have little to do with anything written in Mythic Bastionland. I'll do a post on them later.
